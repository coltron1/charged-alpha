📊 Understanding ETF Screening Results

  • Expense Ratio: The annual fee you pay to hold the ETF. Under 0.1% is ultra-low cost (e.g. VOO, VTI). Over 0.5% starts to eat into returns over time.
  • AUM (Assets Under Management): Larger funds ($1B+) have tighter bid-ask spreads and are less likely to close. Avoid very small ETFs for core holdings.
  • Dividend Yield: The annual income you receive as a percentage of the price. 2-4% is moderate. Above 5% can signal higher risk or a declining price.
  • 52-Week Performance: Shows how much the ETF has gained from its yearly low. Near the 52-week high = strong momentum. Well below = potentially a value buy or a warning sign.
