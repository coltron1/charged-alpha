How to use the Mutual Fund Screener

This tool helps you narrow a curated mix of mutual funds across core stock funds, balanced funds, bond funds, and international funds. Use it to compare common long-term portfolio building blocks by cost, size, yield, risk, and historical return profile in one place.

1. Start broad

Use a preset like Low Cost Core, International Core, or Bond Defense to load a quick starting point.

2. Narrow the list

Search by fund name or ticker, then refine with filters like expense ratio, AUM, return thresholds, category, asset mix, and management style.

3. Compare finalists

Select up to 3 funds with Add to Compare, then open the comparison tray to see returns, fees, risk, turnover, and the winner summary side by side.

Tip: Click any result row for a quick inline summary, or open the full detail view to review the chart, portfolio mix, fund description, and then add or remove that fund from the comparison set.

📊 Understanding Mutual Fund Screening Results

  • Expense Ratio: Lower ongoing costs leave more of the return in your pocket. This matters most for core long-term holdings.
  • Morningstar Rating + Risk: A star rating can help surface stronger past risk-adjusted funds, while the risk rating helps you avoid strategies that may swing harder than you want.
  • Turnover: Higher turnover means the manager trades more often. That can be fine for active strategies, but lower turnover often points to a steadier process.
  • Track Record: Funds with 5-10+ years of live history are easier to judge through multiple market environments.
  • Portfolio Fit: Stock/bond/cash allocation helps you see whether a fund really matches the role you want it to play in a portfolio.
  • Open the detail view for the full breakdown: description, style, ratings, asset mix, holdings, sectors, and bond credit exposure where available.

How to read the highlights

  • Not every metric has a universal “best,” so portfolio-fit fields like strategy and category are shown without forced winners.
